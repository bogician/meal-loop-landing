## Epic Summary

A visitor reads the site in their language — Ukrainian or English — chosen automatically on first visit from `Accept-Language`, remembered on return, and switchable at any time, with every word served from translation catalogs. Epic 1 established the localized `app/[locale]` route tree and `proxy.ts` that every other v2 capability renders inside.

## What Went Well

- **Front-loaded spine paid off.** 1.1 was deliberately the heaviest story; as a result 1.2 was a one-line config change, 1.3/1.4 were catalog fills, and 1.5 was a single client island. Each story opened by confirming "the plumbing already exists — do not re-plumb." Architectural complexity was paid once, not five times.
- **"Verify against the installed package" discipline held.** The `AGENTS.md` mandate ("this is NOT the Next.js you know") caught real divergences from training data: `proxy.ts` not `middleware.ts`, stable `setRequestLocale` (not `unstable_`), and the next-intl 4.x session-cookie default that Story 1.2 existed to fix.
- **Adversarial review caught genuine bugs, not noise.** Shallow message-merge that would have broken per-key FR-4 fallback (1.1); missing `Secure` flag on `NEXT_LOCALE` where the Dev Note had wrongly claimed next-intl set it (1.2); App Store label mood mismatch (1.4). Project-aware layers correctly dismissed diff-blind false positives.
- **Per-key `en` fallback proved robust** — verified by smoke test (delete a `uk` key → English value renders at string granularity, siblings stay Ukrainian).

## What Was Hard

- **Story 1.4 font blocker (the significant discovery).** DESIGN.md, architecture.md, epics.md, prd.md, and project-context.md all specified loading Outfit with a `cyrillic` subset. Outfit ships no Cyrillic (`Array<'latin' | 'latin-ext'>`). The false premise propagated through every planning doc unchallenged and halted the story. Resolved by Bogdan's decision to swap the brand font site-wide to Manrope (variable, Cyrillic-capable geometric sans). This superseded AC-5 (`/en` byte-for-byte unchanged) and AC-6 (scope), both reconciled by explicit approval.
- **Headless env cannot close responsive ACs.** Every i18n story left the manual responsive pass (320/360/390/768) and live click-through unverifiable. This is a structural class of AC the agent cannot close — not a one-off. Real risk parked on it: loop-diagram pill overflow at 320px with longer Ukrainian labels (`Повторювати` 11ch), and the 1.5 nav fit at 320px on `/uk`.

---

## Key Insights

1. Pay architectural complexity once, in the spine story — downstream stories become near-trivial.
2. Planning-doc assumptions about third-party capabilities (font subsets, library APIs) must be verified against the installed package, or they detonate mid-implementation.
3. When a hard decision supersedes written ACs (the font swap), the docs that encoded the old assumption become a latent landmine for every later epic that reads them.
4. A headless dev env structurally cannot satisfy responsive/visual ACs — these must be batched to a human/browser verification stage rather than re-flagged story by story.
